The Regions

One platform.
Three hemispheres.

From Manila to Manama, from Seoul to Singapore — Hack East spans the world's fastest-growing digital economies and the security talent that protects them. The Eastern hemisphere is not catching up with the West. In many domains, it is leading.

🌏

Philippines, Singapore, Vietnam, Thailand, Indonesia — a region of 700M people building digital infrastructure at extraordinary speed.

🌐

East Asia

🕌

Middle East

UAE, Saudi Arabia, Qatar, Jordan — a region investing massively in digital transformation with acute awareness of the cyber risks that come with it.

🏔️

Programs

Built for the
Eastern context

Hack East programmes are designed for the regulatory environments, threat landscapes, and talent pipelines specific to each region — not Western content localised after the fact.

Southeast Asia

ASEAN Cyber Cup

The flagship CTF competition for Southeast Asian nations — contested by university teams from all 10 ASEAN member states each year.

East Asia

Korean & Japanese Red Teams

Advanced offensive security programmes developed with South Korean KITRI and Japanese IPA partners — for the specific threat actors facing these markets.

Middle East

Arab Security League

An Arabic-language cybersecurity training and competition platform — developing regional talent without requiring English proficiency as a barrier to entry.

Pan-Regional

Hack East Summit

An annual gathering bringing together security professionals, researchers, and government representatives from across the Eastern hemisphere.

Industry

Eastern Talent Exchange

A talent placement platform connecting Hack East community members with security roles at regional governments and multinational enterprises operating in the East.

Research

Asia-Pacific Threat Intel

Shared threat intelligence feeds specific to Eastern hemisphere threat actors, APT groups, and regional attack patterns — unavailable from Western-centric vendors.
